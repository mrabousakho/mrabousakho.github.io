THE JOURNEY

My path into cybersecurity started over two decades ago. After graduating in 2001, a professor invited me to help build one of the first educational computer labs and cyber cafés in Guinea, West Africa — a 50-workstation environment I ended up managing for two years: installs, hardware, networking, users, all of it. That's where I learned every technical problem has a root cause waiting to be found.

Back in the U.S., I went deep on enterprise networking — preparing directly for CCIE-level routing and switching. I paused before the final exam, but that prep still shapes how I read traffic and think about how attackers exploit it today.

I earned my M.S. in Cybersecurity from the University of Houston–Downtown in 2020 with a 4.0 GPA. When COVID cut my planned internship short, I built home labs instead — offense, defense, Active Directory, Sentinel, Defender — and kept that going while running my own computer repair business.

In 2025 I joined the LogNPacific Cyber Range for structured, hands-on SOC work. Since then I've investigated 100+ security incidents, written and refined 1,000+ KQL/SPL queries, and used AI to generate and validate 2,000+ more across dozens of threat hunting scenarios — over 200 hours logged.

I've also built four autonomous AI security agents — threat intelligence, security news aggregation, network traffic monitoring, and vulnerability assessment — plus 5+ SOC dashboards for real-time visibility, and I've picked up multiple cash awards in Cyber Range threat hunting competitions along the way.

Right now my focus is securing agentic AI — most organizations are adopting autonomous AI agents faster than they're securing them, and I think that gap is where cybersecurity is headed next.
